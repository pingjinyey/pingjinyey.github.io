Zhisheng Niu received his B.E. degree from Beijing Jiaotong University, China, in 1985, and his M.E. and Ph.D. degrees from Toyohashi University of Technology, Japan, in 1989 and 1992, respectively. After working at Fujitsu Laboratories Ltd., Japan (1992–1994), he joined Tsinghua University in 1994 as a Lecturer, became an Associate Professor later that year, and has been a Professor since 1999. He served as Deputy Dean for Research, School of Information Science and Technology (2004–2016), Deputy Head for Research, Department of Electronic Engineering (2003–2004), and Deputy Director of the University Office of Science and Technology (2000–2002).

His professional visiting experience includes Distinguished Visiting Scholar at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (Shenzhen) (2023–2024), Visiting Scholar at Hamburg University of Technology, Germany and Chalmers University, Sweden (2023), Distinguished Visiting Fellow at Ming-Hsieh Institute, University of Southern California (2014), and Visiting Senior Researcher at Hitachi Central Research Laboratory, Japan (1997–1998).

He is an IEEE Fellow (Class of 2012, "for contributions to collaborative radio resource management in wireless networks") and IEICE Fellow (Class of 2007, "for contributions on network engineering and IEICE globalization"). He served as IEEE ComSoc Distinguished Lecturer (2012–2015) and IEEE Vehicular Technology Society Distinguished Lecturer (2014–2018).

### Graduate Students Supervised

Prof. Niu has graduated 34 Ph.D. and 27 Master students in total, among them 9 found faculty positions in the US, Europe, and China. He has also collaborated with 7 postdoctoral researchers, 5 of whom found faculty positions after completion. He currently supervises a research group of 5 Ph.D. and 2 Master students.
